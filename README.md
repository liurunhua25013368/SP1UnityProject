# Before It Happens (2D)

> For Sprint 1, I worked from a tutorial version of Space Invaders and used it as the basis for my own rule-based intervention. The original game is based on shooting, survival, score, accuracy, and gradually increasing difficulty. Players are encouraged to improve through repeated practice: the more skilful they are, the longer they survive and the higher their score becomes.

In my version, I challenge the assumption that optimal play should exist. In many arcade games, players believe there is always a best strategy: shoot accurately, avoid enemies, collect points, and survive. I wanted to design a system where every strategy has unintended consequences. The player can still collect points, but points are no longer only a sign of success. They can also be converted into life. This creates a dilemma: should the player save points for a higher score, or spend them early to buy more survival time?

This idea was inspired by a real-life experience. Last week my phone was stolen, and because I did not buy AppleCare, I could not get compensation. When I bought the phone, I did not expect it to be stolen, so I did not pay extra for protection. Only after the accident happened did I regret the decision. I wanted to translate this hesitation into a game mechanic.
